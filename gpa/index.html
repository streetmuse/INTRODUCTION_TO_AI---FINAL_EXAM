ILIA STATE UNIVERSITY — GPA Calculation Rule (Translated)

  1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum of the Grade Points (GP) earned in each course multiplied by that course's credits, divided by the total credits the student has taken:
    GPA = Σ (GP of each course × credits of each course) / Σ (credits of each course)
  2. GP (Grade Point) is the score received in a given course, converted to a 4.0 scale.
  3. Under the GP system, scores fall into these categories:
    F — 0–50 points (0 GP, Fail)
    E — 51–60 points
    D — 61–70 points
    C — 71–80 points
    B — 81–90 points
    A — 91–95 points
    A+ — 96–100 points
  4. A score in category E (51–60) is always equal to 1.00 GP, regardless of the exact score. Scores in categories D, C, B, and A (61–95 points) increase evenly, in increments of 1/12 (≈0.08) per point. A score in category A+ (96–100) is fixed at 4.00 GP.
  5. GPA is awarded for every academic program at Ilia State University except doctoral studies, and is not awarded for certificate programs.
  6. Ilia State University issues only a final (cumulative) GPA, shown on the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the single-cycle Medical Doctor program.
  7. When calculating the final GPA, fractions are rounded to one decimal place.
